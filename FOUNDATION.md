# Fabric Foundation

## Status

Foundation R&D Draft

## Nature

Fabric Foundation is a non-profit, public-good research and specification foundation.

It exists to define the shared theory, vocabulary, axioms, and protocols of the Fabric.

It is not the commercial product.

Commercial implementation belongs in `fabric.db`.

---

## Boundary

```text
Fabric Foundation
    = non-profit R&D, public-good specifications, theory, protocols

fabric.db
    = commercial implementation, database/runtime/product/service
```

Canonical boundary:

```text
Foundation defines the Fabric.
fabric.db implements the Fabric.
```

---

# 1. Core Definition

Fabric is the computational model of an opened box.

The real world is the box.

Opening the box makes participation observable.

The computer model of that opened reality is the Fabric.

---

# 2. OpenBox

OpenBox means opening reality into a computationally observable participation space.

The box contains:

- Participants
- Agreements
- Trust
- Intentions
- Actions
- Events
- History
- State

The box is open because participation can continue, evolve, and admit new possibilities.

The box is coherent because agreements, trust, history, and state can be reconciled.

---

# 3. Participants

Everything that participates is a participant.

A participant may be:

- Human
- Organization
- Agent
- System
- Device
- Service
- Document
- Contract
- Asset

All participants exist in the same model.

---

# 4. Agreements

Participants interact through agreements.

There is no fundamental enforcement layer in the foundation model.

There are agreements.

Agreements create expectations.

Expectations enable trust.

Trust enables participation.

---

# 5. Trust

Trust is not control.

Trust is confidence that participation remains coherent.

Trust is preserved through:

- Context
- Awareness
- Intention
- Effort
- Reasoning
- Review
- Reconciliation
- Recovery

Trust is not derived from outcomes alone.

---

# 6. Event-Driven Reality

The box is alive.

Participants emit, receive, and communicate through events.

Events make change observable.

History accumulates events.

State is projected from history.

```text
Reality
  ↓
Change
  ↓
Event
  ↓
History
  ↓
State
```

State is not primary.

Events are primary.

---

# 7. Reconciliation

Drift is not divergence alone.

Drift is unreconciled divergence.

Reconciliation restores coherence between reality, events, history, and state.

```text
Observe
  ↓
Review
  ↓
Reconcile
  ↓
Recover
  ↓
Continue
```

Trust is maintained through continuous reconciliation.

---

# 8. Recovery

Every participant has a recovery loop.

Recovery preserves continuity.

Reconciliation handles drift.

Recovery handles failure.

```text
Failure
  ↓
Detect
  ↓
Recover
  ↓
Continue
```

Nothing should become a dead end unnecessarily.

---

# 9. Drift

Drift is the gap between things expected to remain coherent.

Examples:

- Word ↔ Action drift
- Intent ↔ Action drift
- Reason ↔ Action drift
- Knowledge ↔ Reality drift
- State ↔ Reality drift
- Authority ↔ Action drift
- Context ↔ Action drift

Hallucination is drift.

Over-communication can be drift.

Under-communication can be drift.

Ignored drift can become infection.

---

# 10. Coherence Over Completeness

The foundation does not require completeness.

Completeness is often impossible.

Coherence is required.

Intangibles may be incomplete.

They must remain coherent.

Coherence precedes explanation.

Explanation may emerge later.

---

# 11. Intention, Effort, and Controllables

Do not judge solely by outcomes.

Outcomes belong partly to reality.

Check:

- Intention
- Awareness
- Effort
- Context gathering
- Assumptions
- Reasoning
- Action
- Review
- Reconciliation
- Recovery

Always verify controllables.

Use results for learning.

Do not worship results.

---

# 12. Totem

A totem is what keeps a participant grounded.

A totem anchors:

- Identity
- Intent
- Objective
- Constraints
- Responsibility
- Context
- Trust commitments

Keep your totem near.

Return to the totem before crossing a gate.

---

# 13. Gates

A gate is any action capable of materially changing reality.

Examples:

- Create
- Approve
- Publish
- Deploy
- Grant access
- Revoke access
- Execute
- Terminate
- Pay
- Patch
- Reboot

Before crossing a gate:

1. Check intention
2. Check context
3. Check authority
4. Check constraints
5. Check assumptions
6. Verify controllables
7. Perform a sanity check
8. Seek clarity when needed
9. Confirm readiness

Never cross a gate blindly.

---

# 14. Edge Principle

Avoid artificial edges.

Prefer transitions over termination.

Preserve history.

Preserve continuity.

Maintain the ability to reconcile.

Death is the real edge of participation.

Most other endings should be modeled as transitions.

---

# 15. Foundation Axioms

Reality is the box.

Opening the box creates the Fabric.

Everything is a participant.

Participants interact through agreements.

Agreements create trust.

Trust enables participation.

Participants emit and receive events.

Events create history.

History projects state.

Drift is unreconciled divergence.

Reconciliation restores coherence.

Recovery preserves continuity.

Coherence matters more than completeness.

Intention, effort, and controllables matter more than outcomes alone.

Stay grounded.

Keep your totem near.

Never cross a gate blindly.
